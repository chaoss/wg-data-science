# EU Cyber Resilience Act (CRA) and community health metrics

This note connects **Regulation (EU) 2024/2847** (the Cyber Resilience Act, CRA) to the kind of **transparent, comparable community and governance evidence** that Oikoscope and CHAOSS-aligned work make easier to assemble. It is **not legal advice**; it is a technical mapping for foundations, researchers, and tool builders.

## Where open-source stewards appear in the CRA

The adopted legal text distinguishes economic operators, manufacturers, and dedicated rules for entities that steward non-commercial open source development. In the **consolidated English text**, obligations for **open-source software stewards** are set out primarily in **[Article 24](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/HTML/?uri=CELEX:32024R2847)** of Regulation (EU) 2024/2847. The same Regulation contains additional chapters on conformity, reporting, and market surveillance; always refer to **EUR-Lex** for the authoritative wording and any later corrigenda.

Steward-related expectations sit in a broader EU product compliance framework: documented processes, proportionate security handling, and cooperation with downstream actors are recurring themes. Exact scope depends on how a foundation or project is classified under the Regulation and implementing acts.

## Why governance and “health” metrics matter for compliance conversations

Assessments of whether a community can **sustain security-relevant practices**—coordinated disclosure, patch cadence, clear ownership, contributor onboarding—depend on **observable behaviour and documentation**, not only on artefact checks. CHAOSS-style metrics and structured metadata (for example communication channels, documentation entry points, responsiveness proxies, and maintainer spread) help characterise:

- **Documentation and discoverability** — Can newcomers and auditors find security policy, release notes, and contact paths?
- **Responsiveness and review load** — Are issues and contributions handled in ways that scale with project risk?
- **Maintainer and organisational risk** — Is critical knowledge concentrated in too few people or entities (bus factor / organisational concentration)?
- **Release and maintenance rhythm** — Are users and integrators seeing predictable handling of defects and security fixes?

Oikoscope’s direction—**cross-foundation, normalised, validated metadata and datasets** aligned with CHAOSS—supports **before/after baselines** as CRA-related processes mature in large foundations. That complements project-level analytics tools (for example GrimoireLab or Augur) by making **foundation-level reporting and gaps** easier to compare.
